Becoming an Inclusive Designer

When starting my education as an UX/UI Designer I learned about empathy maps, user flows, color theory and so much more. When it came to learning about inclusive design I struggled to find resources on how as a designer I could implement accessibility features and inclusion into the technology I would be designing from it's creation. I was directed towards contrast checker and minimum font sizes but having spent time volunteering with The Special Olympics and Achilles Athletes I knew we needed to do much more. I also realized if I was struggling to find this information that others are too; that struggle could lead to individuals being discouraged from creating potentially life changing programs. As I continue my journey to become an inclusive designer I would love for you to come along too and if you already are an inclusive and accessibility oriented designer I would love to learn from you.Please send me an email with the contact link above.
